## What I learned (concepts)
- **Supervised → unsupervised shift.** No answer column; the structure *is* the answer. Two families today: clustering (group similar rows) and dimensionality reduction (compress correlated features).
- **A row is one point across all features at once.** Distance collapses every feature into a single number (sum of squared gaps, square-rooted). Features never cluster separately. This is why scaling is load-bearing — every feature throws its squared gap into the same sum.
- **k-means = assign↔update loop.** Drop k centroids, assign each point to nearest centroid, move each centroid to its members' mean, repeat until nothing moves. Points are compared to **k centroids, not to each other** — the reason it scales where hierarchical clustering (point-to-point) doesn't.
- **Choosing k is judgement, not a formula.** Inertia always falls → find the elbow (the bend). Silhouette has a real peak → take the max. Business need (segments you can act on) breaks ties. Documenting *why* is the skill.
- **PCA finds directions of maximum spread (variance), ranked.** Correlated features are redundant; PCA collapses them into combined components. Two distinct uses: (1) preprocessing to beat the curse of dimensionality, (2) flatten to 2D for visualization.
- **Curse of dimensionality:** in many dimensions, all points drift toward equidistant, so "nearest" loses meaning — hurts both k-means and (later) RAG retrieval. PCA is therapy for it.
- **t-SNE / UMAP are visualize-only** — they distort global geometry and aren't a reusable transform, so never feed them downstream. PCA is the reduce-*and*-reuse one.
