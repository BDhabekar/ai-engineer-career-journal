**Key concepts learned:**
- Normal equation vs. gradient descent: same answer, different scaling tradeoff (matrix inversion vs. iterative steps).
- Batch / SGD / mini-batch is a noise-vs-cost tradeoff, not a correctness difference.
- Logistic regression's gradient has the *same form* as linear regression's, with the prediction run through sigmoid — a natural future "from scratch" exercise.
- L1 drives weights to exactly zero (sparsity / feature selection); L2 only shrinks them smoothly.
- Two-pass vs. one-pass is a style / constant-factor distinction, not a complexity-class one — both were O(n).
- Combining weights and bias into one vector (via a bias column on X) and keeping them separate are mathematically identical — the difference is readability, not correctness.

**Result / metric:** Gradient descent matched the direct solution and sklearn to 4 decimals (final cost 0.2988 after 200 iterations). Lasso correctly zeroed 6 of 10 known-zero weights.
